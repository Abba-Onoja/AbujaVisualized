Urban Atlas

The Abuja atlas is an ongoing guide to the people, places and geography of Nigeria's capital. It brings together neighborhoods, districtss, landmarks, parks, markets, institutions and public spaces into one resource. Through maps, data and stories, the Atlas helps explain not just where places are, but why they matter and how they fit into the larger story of Abuja.

Informal Food Map

Some of Abuja's best meals aren't inside a restaurant. This community-built project documents roadside vendors, bukas, masa sellers, suya spots, kiosks and hidden local favourites across the city. Know a great suya spot? A roadside akara seller? A hidden buka? Help document Abuja's informal food scene by adding local vendors that deserve to be on the map.

Service Gap Finder
for Abuja

A lot of residential areas in Abuja — particularly the satellite towns — lack access to basic everyday services like laundry and last-mile logistics. This project maps those gaps. It identifies neighbourhoods where residents have to travel unreasonable distances to access a simple service, using publicly available location data and proximity analysis. The goal is a clear, visual picture of where service demand exists but supply does not.

Abuja
Business Report

Nigerian agricultural policy is scattered across government portals that most people never read. This Streamlit app pulls from those sources, aggregates the relevant documents, and uses a language model to surface plain-English summaries of new policies, regulatory changes, and funding announcements. The intended user is a farmer, cooperative, or agribusiness that needs to stay informed without spending hours parsing government language.
